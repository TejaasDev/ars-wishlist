// why we exist

Robotics found us too late.
We're fixing that.

Most students in rural India never hear the word "robotics" until college — if at all. The tools are too complex. The resources are in English. The hardware is expensive. And the developers who understand it all charge more than most startups can afford.

RoboPilot Labs was started by a 16-year-old who discovered robotics late and decided that no student — rural or urban, rich or middle-class — should have that same regret.

"I found robotics too late. I'm building RoboPilot Labs so no one else has to say that."

— Founder, RoboPilot Labs · Class 11, India

We are building the platform that makes robotics as accessible as making a website. If you can describe it, RoboPilot can build it.

// the problem

Robotics is broken.
Here's how.

Three walls stand between people and building robots. We're tearing all three down.

01 // SKILL

Too hard to start

ROS, C++, embedded systems, wiring diagrams — just to make a robot move forward. The learning curve kills most dreams before they begin.

02 // COST

Developers cost a fortune

A good robotics engineer costs ₹80L–₹1.2Cr per year. Most startups and students simply cannot afford to hire one. So the idea dies.

03 // ACCESS

Rural India is left out

The best robotics education reaches metro cities and IITs. A student in a tier-3 town has almost no way in. That talent is being wasted.
